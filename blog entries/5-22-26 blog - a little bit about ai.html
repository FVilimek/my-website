5/22/2026 - A little bit about AI

Image: An AI-generated image of a humanoid robot, a "classical" personification of AI. Credit: University of Rochester.

AI can be incredibly useful... if used correctly

We are now in a strange time in human history. We have advanced far enough that we can essentially construct non-sentient objects that can almost think for themselves. Based on matrix operations in neural networks, this artificial intelligence has grown exponentially over the years. Gaining popularity with OpenAI's LLM ChatGPT, a number of other LLMs have become available for the public to use. AI assistants are being added to various websites and softwares with the goal of making your life easier. AI is being used for front-end and back-end software development, hardware design, business decisions, teaching, even research-level academic work. I hate saying this as much as some people hate to hear it, but AI is incredibly powerful and can be equally as useful... if used correctly.

I oppose the use of AI just as much as, if not more than, the next guy. Something about having to delegate something that I can do with my own brain to some collection of ones and zeros bothers me to no end, but then again, I take a great deal of pride in doing things myself and figuring out how it works---the rush of excitement once I finally get it working without much external assistance is truly addicting. In my undergraduate years, when the AI boom was starting to ramp, I was already in opposition of how AI was being used in its nascent form with solving homework problems, writing short essays, those sorts of things. Now with the AI boom being so strong we're risking the future of our planet as we know it, I am in great opposition of the scale of things. We're truly living in the bad timeline.

Now don't get me wrong, in the previous paragraph I said I'm in great opposition of the direction the AI boom is going, but if we were to evaluate AI purely on its ability to streamline workflows and allow people to be overall more productive, I think the tech CEOs are right, AI is super powerful. Now also remember that AI does not just mean LLMs, I consider anything that operates on neural networks to be some form of AI. Expanding to this definition of AI, there are literally uncountable ways that AI can be used to solve complex problems. Consider my current area of research, fault-tolerant controls, which relies heavily on numerical optimization. Some problems are incredibly difficult to solve with one equation or one algorithm, because there are too many parameters to calculate or too much uncertainty in measurements. However, with a neural network, you can feed it a bunch of data from simulations and it will be able to solve the problem for you.

Why think for yourself if AI can do it for you?
